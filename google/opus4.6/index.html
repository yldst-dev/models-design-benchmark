Think of it this way: classical computers process bits — either 0 or 1. Quantum computers use "qubits" that can be 0, 1, or both at the same time (superposition), letting them explore many solutions simultaneously. This makes them exponentially faster for specific problems like drug discovery and cryptography. ✨

Our Journey

25+ Years of Innovation

From a Stanford dorm room to one of the world's most impactful technology companies.

1998

Google is Founded

Larry Page and Sergey Brin incorporate Google Inc. in Menlo Park, CA, with a mission to organize the world's information.

2004

Gmail & IPO

Gmail launches with 1 GB of storage — revolutionary at the time. Google goes public at $85/share, raising $1.67B.

2008

Chrome & Android

Google Chrome launches and quickly becomes the world's most popular browser. Android 1.0 ships on the HTC Dream.

2015

Alphabet Restructure

Google reorganizes under Alphabet Inc., enabling moonshot projects like Waymo and Verily to operate with greater independence.

2023

The AI Era Begins

Bard launches and evolves into Gemini — Google's most capable multimodal AI, integrated across all major products.

2024

Quantum Supremacy 2.0

Google's Willow quantum chip achieves exponential error reduction, marking a historic milestone in computing.
